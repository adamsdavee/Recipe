Fufu and Egusi Soup

A delicious West African delicacy enjoyed across Nigeria and beyond. The soft, stretchy fufu pairs perfectly with the rich, nutty, and flavorful egusi soup. This hearty meal is best shared with family and friends.

Recipe Information

Prep time: 20 minutes

Cooking time: 50 minutes

Servings: 6 people

Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 2 cups ground egusi (melon seeds)
  • 500g assorted meat (meat or chicken)
  • 2 cooking spoons palm oil
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons ground crayfish
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • vegetable leaves (ugu or bitterleaf)
  • 2 fresh peppers (blended)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups cassava flour (for fufu)

Instructions

  1. In a pot, season and cook the assorted meat until tender. Set aside.
  2. Heat palm oil in a large pot and add chopped onions. Fry lightly.
  3. Stir in the ground egusi and fry until it thickens into a paste.
  4. Add the blended pepper, crayfish, seasoning cubes, and meat stock. Stir well.
  5. Introduce the cooked meat and allow the soup to simmer for about 20 minutes.
  6. Add the vegetable leaves and cook for another 5 minutes. Adjust seasoning with salt.
  7. Meanwhile, prepare the fufu: mix cassava flour with hot water and stir until smooth and stretchy.
  8. Serve the hot egusi soup with freshly prepared fufu. Enjoy!

Tips & Notes

For richer flavor, you can add smoked fish or stockfish to your egusi soup. Do not overcook the vegetables to preserve their nutrients and fresh taste.
